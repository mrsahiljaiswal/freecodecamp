Glimpse of His divine life

1896 - Born in Calcutta, India

Abhay Charan De, later known as Srila Prabhupada, was born on September 1, 1896, in Calcutta, India. His parents were prominent Vaishnavas, and his early upbringing in a devout household instilled a deep spiritual awareness in him.

1922 - Meets his Guru, Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Sarasvati Thakur

In 1922, Srila Prabhupada met his spiritual master, Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Sarasvati Thakur, a revered leader of the Gaudiya Vaishnavism tradition. Srila Bhakti Siddhanta encouraged him to spread the teachings of Lord Chaitanya to the Western world.

1923 - Takes formal initiation

He formally became a disciple of Srila Bhakti Siddhanta Sarasvati Thakur and received the name Abhay Charan. His commitment to spiritual life deepened under the guidance of his guru.

1933 - Starts the ‘Back to Godhead’ Magazine

In 1933, Srila Prabhupada began publishing the magazine Back to Godhead, a publication dedicated to promoting the teachings of Lord Chaitanya and spreading the message of devotional service. This magazine would become a key tool in his future mission to the West.

1944 - Initiates the Mission to Spread the Teachings

Srila Prabhupada's long-standing desire to spread the teachings of Lord Chaitanya to the West intensified, and in 1944, he formally began writing his translations and commentaries of the sacred texts.

1959 - Establishes the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON)

After years of spiritual practice, study, and writing, Srila Prabhupada formalized his mission by establishing the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) in 1966 in New York City, a global movement dedicated to the teachings of Lord Krishna and Bhakti Yoga.

1965 - Journey to the United States

In 1965, at the age of 69, Srila Prabhupada sailed to the United States, alone and with minimal resources, to spread the teachings of Lord Krishna. His determination to fulfill his guru's mission overcame many obstacles, and he arrived in New York in September 1965.

1966 - Establishment of ISKCON

Upon arrival in New York, Srila Prabhupada began to gather followers and lay the foundation for ISKCON. His deep spiritual wisdom, humility, and sincere service attracted many, and the movement began to grow.

1968 - Establishes the First Temple in London

Srila Prabhupada’s efforts spread to Europe, where he opened the first ISKCON temple in London. His followers grew rapidly, with temples opening in major cities across the world, including Los Angeles, San Francisco, and London.

1972 - Initiates the First Batch of Western Disciples

Srila Prabhupada began initiating the first group of Western disciples, which was a significant moment for the Gaudiya Vaishnavism tradition, as it marked the expansion of the movement beyond India.

1977 - Srila Prabhupada Passes Away

On November 14, 1977, Srila Prabhupada passed away, leaving behind an incredible legacy. His followers continued his work, spreading the message of Krishna consciousness worldwide.
